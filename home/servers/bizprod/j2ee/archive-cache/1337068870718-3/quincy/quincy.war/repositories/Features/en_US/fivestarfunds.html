Quincy's four five star funds offer an easy way to achieve diversification. Because you invest in hundreds or even thousands of securities, broad diversification helps to cushion your portfolio from the ups and downs of the financial markets. If you look at what each of these funds offer, you'll see why Morningstar rated them five star funds.

The Quincy High Yield Bond Fund
Fund Type: Bond - Corporate
Objective: Quincy High Yield Fund seeks high current income; long-term capital appreciation is a secondary goal.
Strategy: The Fund invests primarily in bonds, other debt securities, and preferred stocks issued by corporations. Fund holdings focus primarily on obligations rated in the lower credit categories by established ratings services. Up to 25% of the Fund's total assets may be invested in dollar-denominated foreign debt securities, which are subject to special risks, such as differences in securities regulation and accounting, as well as currency fluctuations.

The Quincy Growth Fund
Fund Type: Growth
Objective: Quincy Growth Fund seeks long-term capital growth, with a secondary goal of current income.
Strategy: The Fund invests primarily in larger-capitalization common stocks selected for a history of consistent earnings growth regardless of the business cycle. A lesser but important emphasis is placed on companies with accelerating earnings, revenues, and strong cash flows. The Fund may also invest in foreign securities which are subject to special risks, such as differences in securities and accounting regulations, as well as currency fluctuations.

The Quincy Growth & Income Fund
Fund Type: Growth & Income
Objective: The Fund seeks high total return through a combination of capital appreciation and current income.
Strategy: The Quincy G&I fund places a heavier emphasis on growth while making current income a secondary objective. It targets stocks that fund management believes are cheaply priced relative to their true value. This focus is applied to large companies poised to implement positive change, such as restructuring measures, new products or distribution methods, and cost reductions. This fund has the flexibility to invest in more medium-size companies and holds fewer stocks in more concentrated positions.

The Quincy Venture Fund
Fund Type: Growth Sector-Technology
Objective: Quincy Venture Fund seeks long-term capital appreciation.
Strategy: The Fund invests primarily in the equity securities of companies engaged in technology-related industries such as computers, communications, video, electronics, oceanography, office and factory automation, and robotics. The Fund may invest in foreign securities, which are subject to special risks, such as differences in securities and accounting regulations, as well as currency fluctuations.
